# AI Digest — 2026-05-26

## Product Updates

### Anthropic — Mythos Commercialization, Pentagon Fight, Glasswing Expansion
- **Summary**: Three major Anthropic stories today. (1) Mythos 1 (`claude-mythos-1-preview`) is visibly moving toward public release inside Claude Code and Claude Security — references appeared in public builds before being pulled. A new consumer-facing Security dashboard is being built alongside Claude Code and Claude Design. (2) The Verge reports Anthropic is fighting the Pentagon to preserve two "red lines": no domestic mass surveillance, no fully autonomous lethal weapons. After Anthropic objected, the DoD signed deals with 8 other companies (Google, Microsoft, AWS, Nvidia, OpenAI, Reflection, Oracle, SpaceX). (3) Glasswing expanded: 1,596 vulnerabilities disclosed across 281 open-source projects, 97 patched. Maintainers are overwhelmed — Anthropic partnered with OpenSSF Alpha-Omega for triage support.
- **Source**: https://cybersecuritynews.com/claude-mythos-moves-toward-public/ ; https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/937028/military-ai-warfare-red-lines ; https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/05/26/anthropic-project-glasswing-update/

The Pentagon fight is a defining moment for the industry — other labs (Google, OpenAI) quickly signed the broader terms. Meanwhile, Mythos commercialization is happening in layers: enterprise first, then broader access.

The ranking: Letta for memory-heavy agents, LangGraph for complex production orchestration, Pydantic AI for structured outputs. Letta rated "emerging" on production readiness.

## People & Commentary

### Anthropic vs Pentagon — The Red Line Fight
- **What they said/did**: Anthropic is fighting to preserve bans on domestic mass surveillance and autonomous lethal weapons. Defense Secretary Hegseth demanded broader terms; Anthropic refused. Eight other companies signed on instead.

### Benedict Evans — AI Eats the World 2026
- **What they said/did**: Evans' annual AI deck highlights $700B in hyperscaler AI capex for 2026 (2x global telecom capex), consumer adoption that's wider but shallower, and enterprise deployments still mostly pilots. The big question: does AI demand catch up with AI supply?

- **Context**: The capex numbers are genuinely unprecedented. If demand doesn't catch up, we're looking at a potential credit event. The parallel to the 2000 dot-com infrastructure overbuild is uncomfortable but real.

### AllFaith Benchmark — Religious Bias in AI
- **What they said/did**: Researchers from Baylor, BYU, Notre Dame, and Yeshiva found systematic religious bias across 20 AI models. ChatGPT 4.0 scored last. Grok 4.20 and Mistral 25.12 scored best. AI models subtly steer users toward some faiths and away from others.
- **Source**: https://www.deseret.com/faith/2026/05/26/studies-find-religious-bias-in-ai-models/
- **Context**: Released the day after the Vatican encyclical. Fewer than 0.02% of AI bias studies focus on religion. This is a new frontier for AI safety — not just factual accuracy, but representational fairness.

## New Tools & Discoveries

### LiteLLM Agent Platform
- **What it does**: Open-source Kubernetes layer for running AI coding agents in isolated sandboxes with credential vaulting and persistent session management. Built on kubernetes-sigs/agent-sandbox CRD. Alpha stage.
- **Source**: https://byteiita.com/litellm-agent-platform-run-ai-coding-agents-on-kubernetes/
- **Why notable**: Solves the infrastructure gap for production agent deployments — sandbox lifecycle, per-session credential isolation, and state persistence. The vault proxy (agents never see real credentials) is the key security innovation.

### Kore.ai Artemis on Azure
- **What it does**: Enterprise agent platform with Agent Blueprint Language (ABL) — a declarative language for defining agents. The "Arch" AI converts business objectives into production-ready ABL. Dual-brain architecture for auditable agent behavior. Launched on Microsoft Azure.
- **Source**: https://itbrief.news/story/kore-ai-launches-artemis-ai-platform-on-microsoft-azure-4bc9b2ee-2660-4f61-a093-96fec3675af3
- **Why notable**: ABL could become the intermediate representation for agent definitions — like YAML for infrastructure. If it catches on, it standardizes how agents are specified and shared.

### AI Vulnerability Research Agents
- **What it does**: LLM agents (Claude Mythos Preview, GPT-5.5) autonomously finding zero-days at scale. Cloudflare: 2,000 bugs across 50 repos. Mozilla Firefox: 271 bugs (10x previous). GPT-5.5 delivers Mythos-class performance in white-box scenarios and is publicly available.
- **Source**: https://dev.to/monuminu/llm-agents-are-now-finding-zero-days-how-ai-is-autonomously-rewriting-the-rules-of-vulnerability-3d2i
- **Why notable**: Production security systems, not research prototypes. The practical path: GPT-5.5 in a well-architected harness for most teams, Mythos via Anthropic's Cyber Verification Program for top-tier security orgs.
